Preprint • 2025 • DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.2509.10565

Assessing the Limits of Graph Neural Networks for Vapor–Liquid Equilibrium

Negative-results study on cryogenic mixtures; trained GNN surrogates fail in specific phase regimes.

Abstract: We evaluate graph neural network surrogates for vapor–liquid equilibrium across cryogenic mixture regimes and identify systematic failures near phase boundaries and critical regions. A diagnostic workflow exposes derivative pathologies and instability under extrapolation, motivating hybrid strategies with physics-grounded fallbacks.

  • Derivative pathologies: gradients and Hessians exhibit nonphysical sign flips near two-phase envelopes.
  • Diagnostic workflow: perturbation tests, envelope tracing, and sensitivity sweeps to detect failure modes.
  • Hybrid fallback: switch to thermodynamic EOS or flash calculations when surrogate confidence degrades.
  • Practical guidance: guardrails for deployment in process modeling and cryogenic design studies.

Preprint • 2025 • DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.2508.12170

Energy-Efficient Robotics Software (2020–2024): Systematic Literature Review

Synthesis of 79 studies on energy-aware robotics software.

Abstract: We conduct a systematic review of post-2020 research on energy-efficient robotics software, covering middleware, scheduling, perception, and control stacks. The review proposes a taxonomy, introduces a reporting checklist for power/impact studies, and releases a replication package for reuse.

  • Taxonomy: layers (hardware drivers → middleware → algorithms) and targets (compute, communication, actuation).
  • Reporting checklist: standardized metrics, workloads, and logging protocols for comparability.
  • Gaps: limited cross-platform replication, sparse uncertainty quantification, few long-horizon workloads.
  • Replication package: scripts and data for reproducing the synthesis and figures.

Software • v1.0 • Zenodo DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17489426

ThermoBench-Consist (v1.0)

Benchmark/diagnostic tool for validating ML equations-of-state (EOS) with physics-based consistency checks and guardrails for deployment. Reports are structured, reproducible, and machine-parsable via JSON scoring.

  • Four physics checks (C1–C4): basic thermodynamic identities, convexity/positivity, Maxwell relations, and phase-consistency probes.
  • Report structure: per-test metrics, exception logs, derivative sanity panels, and envelope-trace summaries.
  • Design philosophy: reproducibility by default, explicit assumptions, and conservative scoring to flag edge cases.

REB-1: Robot Energy Benchmark (v0.1)

Micro-benchmark for power and impact logging on robotics workloads; complements ROS tools and emphasizes reproducibility and comparability.

  • CLI features: workload templates, sampling rate controls, synchronized power/telemetry logging, export to CSV/JSON.
  • Reproducibility: manifest-based runs, environment capture, and seed/config registries.
  • Complementarity: integrates alongside ROS/ros2 bags and power meters for end-to-end experiments.

Supplementary Mathematical Work

Abstract: This short technical note demonstrates quantitative modeling and stability analysis methods relevant to thermodynamic and robotic systems. We derive and analyze coupled ODE/PDE models for transport and phase behavior, examine equilibrium and local stability via Jacobian spectra, and discuss well-posedness and sensitivity to parameterization. Illustrative examples include linearized flash dynamics, energy-informed control cost functions, and nondimensionalization for scale separation.

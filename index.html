About

I think of research as engineering that learns. I move between physics, code, and hardware to build tools that make complex systems behave predictably. Whether it’s a carbon-capture model, an automation pipeline, or a robotics benchmark, the question is the same: how do we make systems that measure themselves, adapt, and keep working under constraint?

I work at Technip Energies, where I’ve helped build molecular-simulation and automation pipelines for industrial R&D. Outside work, I design open diagnostic frameworks and benchmarks that test the limits of learned physics models and robotics software. My long-term goal is to link simulation, control, and data into one loop: software that learns from the materials and machines it runs.

About

I think of research as engineering that learns. I move between physics, code, data, and hardware to build tools that make complex systems easier to measure, test, and improve. Whether it is a carbon-capture model, a dynamical-systems diagnostic, an automation pipeline, or a robotics benchmark, the question is the same: how do we make systems that reveal their structure and keep working under constraint?

I work at Technip Energies, where I have built molecular-simulation, data-processing, and AI-assisted engineering workflows for industrial R&D and EPC teams. Outside work, I design open diagnostic frameworks and benchmarks for learned physics models, trajectory analysis, and energy-aware robotics. My long-term goal is to link simulation, control, and data into one loop: software that learns from the materials, machines, and processes it runs.

Independent Research

Where physics meets code

My independent work sits where physics meets code. I build open tools and experiments that expose how models behave and how we can trust them.

Projects

ThermoBench-Consist v1.0 (2025)

Benchmark/diagnostic for validating ML equations of state with four physics checks and JSON scoring; guardrails near critical regions.

  • Why - Accuracy alone can mask violations of monotonicity/stability/Clapeyron/acoustic speed.
  • Approach - Minimal API; assertions emit structured failures that map to a violated identity.
  • Outcome - CI-ready tests that catch physically impossible predictions before deployment.
  • Methods & checks - finite-difference identities; envelope/phase-boundary sanity tests; critical-region clamping.
  • Artifacts - Zenodo DOI

Assessing the Limits of GNNs for Vapor-Liquid Equilibrium (2025)

Negative-results study: seemingly accurate GNNs fail global thermodynamic consistency; proposes a hybrid fallback with classical libraries.

  • Why - Small pointwise error ≠ integrable/consistent fields.
  • Approach - Stress tests with identity checks; trigger a classical fallback when tests fail.
  • Outcome - Simple policy that preserves identities under shift; guidance for combining ML + physics libraries.
  • Methods & checks - global integrability tests, common-tangent/Maxwell constructions, cross-library comparisons.
  • Artifacts - arXiv

Energy-Efficient Robotics Software (2020-2024): Systematic Literature Review (2025)

A 79-study synthesis of post-2020 work on energy in autonomy stacks (planning, perception, middleware, HW acceleration) across mobile and manipulator platforms.

  • Why - Robotics papers report speed/latency, but energy is inconsistently measured and hard to compare.
  • Approach - Systematic screening + taxonomy of energy-aware techniques (scheduling/DVFS, algorithmic refactoring, off-loading, task-level policies) and a reporting checklist.
  • Outcome - Quantified gaps (e.g., Wh/mission, energy-latency trade-offs, standard workloads) and under-measured components (sensing, comms, OS services); released a full replication package that regenerates all figures.
  • Methods & checks - Inclusion/exclusion rationale, screening spreadsheet, reproducible code and data; foundation for REB-1’s Wh→gCO₂ framing and quick-run workloads.
  • Artifacts - arXiv · Zenodo DOI

REB-1: Robot Energy Benchmark v0.1 (2025)

Micro-benchmark for measuring power and carbon footprint of robotics workloads; standardized 60-second traces and tidy logs.

  • Why - Robotics stacks vary widely in energy cost; teams lack a consistent yardstick.
  • Approach - Fixed trace window; normalized telemetry; ready-to-plot summaries.
  • Outcome - Repeatable numbers that enable energy-aware choices in perception/control pipelines.
  • Methods & checks - synchronized power + compute metrics; normalization across hardware.
  • Artifacts - Zenodo DOI
